Transitions

Tumblr wasn’t cutting it. I graduate from Carnegie Mellon in May and, let’s face it, I will need a job. I attended a career fair on campus and seemed to be unique in that I didn’t have an easily accessible portfolio. Oh, I have things that I can show off, but most of my work has either been very academic or under the control of academia.

I have been using my blog to post some short pieces for my game design course, which will now transition over to this blog, but I also intend to show off some of what I can do. Tumblr just isn’t the right outlet for that. I wanted to have more control, yet not pay a dime beyond my URL registration. That’s where the grad student ethos comes in to play.

Instead of paying for a good tumblr theme (they do exist) or paying for hosting, I decided to switch things up. I am now hosting this site on GitHub and developing a small tool using Heroku. All for free. Sweet sweet internet, where would I be without you?

I will transition my game design posts to a section on this (still under construction) site, but they aren’t up there yet. Fear not, that’s why the gods of academia invented spring break.
