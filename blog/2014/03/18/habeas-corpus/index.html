Habeas Corpus

“The problem with big data is _____”

Fill in the blank. Everyone else is these days, it’s nearly as fashionable as actually ‘doing’ big data. I hate jumping on any bandwagon, but I feel the need to contribute to this particular conversation. I’ve seen many amazing, and often pretty, data visualization tools, but a common mistake, one I think happens far too often. Data is awesome, but without expert context it isn’t knowledge. Just having the data doesn’t do you all that much good.

At Carnegie Mellon we have some incredible machine learning projects. The Never Ending Image Learner (http://www.neil-kb.com) is an excellent example, warning, that’s an addictive link. Then there’s pretty much the entirety of the LTI (http://www.lti.cs.cmu.edu/research/projects.shtml). The math behind those projects is nothing short of astonishing. And by astonishing I mean that I haven’t a clue. Oh, I understand enough statistics to use factor analysis to find useful results out of a text based corpus, but I don’t have the decade or two of computational math to really grasp the equations that they use.

Instead I am focusing on augmented analytical reading. By using some of the big data analytic methods to clean up and balance a corpus and then highlight statistically interesting data for an expert in the field to read I believe that we can get far better results and far more knowledge. I’m working on a project to do some of that for my portfolio. It’s in the “well, that sort of works” phase and certainly isn’t ready for me to point at and say “look, cool stuff that I did”, but it will get there.
